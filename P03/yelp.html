Overview

PURPOSE

Yelp is an online business directory where enterprises can get listed and found online. Yelp’s purpose is to provide a way for connecting people to local businesses through community published reviews, ratings, and photos to influence their decision and choice.

TEAM

  • Timothy Chow

RESEARCH

To successfully design a new feature for yelp, we needed to explore and investigate its interface as it is now. Understanding what the primary purpose of Yelp is, Its key application features, the interface design patterns, interaction patterns, as well as their intended users.

We looked into beginner and expert users to understand when and why they use the app. Through interviews, we recognized that majority of users spent most of their time deciding on where they should go.

Design Process

INSIGHTS

Conducting exploratory and evaluative research to find usability issues, we found two major concerns that the users encountered. We decided to focus on our first concern, which addresses the issue of excessive information that yelp provides, particularly within the search process.

Solution

COMPARE FEATURE

A mode within the search results page that lets the user to select potential destinations to compare the local businesses’ information with each other.

What we want to provide:

A function that reduces cognitive overhead by putting relevant information side by side so that the person using Yelp can efficiently make a decision between a limited amount of options.

WHY?

The compare feature is designed, to ultimately assist the users main goal- to decide where to go. Due to the abundance of information that is provided within the search results, the user is required to remember where each business was in the results. The compare feature assists the user as it allows them to select their desired businesses and view them together in a separate location.

REFLECTION

As a user of Yelp, conducting interviews allowed me to see past my own bias to expand our understanding of other users’ challenges. Enabling me to investigate and design to solve other user’s challenges within the app, rather than my own. Working under a time constraint allowed us to gather important information quickly. We learned to reject good ideas that did not fit within the scope, allowing us to consider all possibilities before choosing the right one.
